Platform engineers are commanding $172K-$207K in 2026, a 13-27% premium over DevOps roles. This episode breaks down salary benchmarks from Dice, Motion Recruitment, and Levels.fyi, revealing which skills are S-tier ($200K+) and which are table stakes.

## Salary Benchmarks 2026

| Role | Average Salary | Top Earners | Source |
|------|----------------|-------------|--------|
| **Platform Engineer** | $172K-$207K | $250K+ | Motion Recruitment 2026 |
| **DevOps Engineer** | $140K-$152K | $216K | Dice 2025 |
| **AI/ML Engineer** | $206K | $312K | Second Talent |
| **LLM Developer** | $209K | $300K+ | Motion 2026 |
| **DevSecOps Engineer** | $180K | $295K (90th %ile) | Glassdoor |
| **FinOps Engineer** | $128K | $175K+ | ZipRecruiter |

## Premium Percentages

- **Platform Engineer vs DevOps**: 13-27% premium
- **AI expertise premium**: 17.7% higher than non-AI roles
- **DevOps with Go expertise**: 25-35% higher rates
- **Cloud certifications add**: $10K-$25K annually

## Recommendations by Level

| Level | Focus | Target Skills |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| **Junior (0-3 yrs)** | Specialize early | Pick one: K8s, Go, or Python + cloud |
| **Mid (3-7 yrs)** | Compound specialization | Platform Engineering + IDP or MLOps |
| **Senior (7+ yrs)** | Staff-level positions | DevSecOps, AI Infrastructure, Staff Platform Engineer |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Specialization beats generalization** - Entry-level generalist roles shrinking, specialized roles growing
2. **AI is a force multiplier, not a replacement** - Learn to work with AI, not compete against it
3. **Platform Engineering premium persists** - 13-27% over DevOps, expected to continue
4. **Compound skills create multipliers** - DevSecOps, MLOps create premium on premium
5. **The fundamentals remain constant** - Invest in skills that compound, not skills that commoditize
